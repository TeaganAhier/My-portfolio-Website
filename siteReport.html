Reflective Discussion of My Module Experience

During my time studying web development, I have learnt HTML, CSS, and a small amount of JavaScript. With this, I have created a portfolio which currently displays placeholder projects, but I will switch out with my own ones throughout my time in university and in this report, I will discuss the positives and negatives which I faced when creating it.

How I Found the Module

I really enjoyed the design aspect of creating my portfolio website. CSS allowed me to add visuals to the site, which I found very rewarding. However, there were some challenges along the way, especially when it came to aligning elements and ensuring that the layout looked good across different devices. At times, I got stuck trying to make sure everything was responsive and well-structured but by experimenting with different solutions, I was able to fix the issues and achieve the design I was aiming for. Overall, the process was a bit tricky, but it was a great learning experience and I feel proud of the end result.

Design Decisions

During a recent visit to an American diner, I really liked the retro design elements as it gave the place such a unique vibe. The neon signs, bold typography, and overall colourful layout made me take inspiration and incorporate some of these elements into my portfolio. I took inspiration from the diner to use a checkerboard border that ran across the top and bottom of the page. (Ai, 2025). As well as generating my own picture for the project page. (Ai, 2025)

I came across a neon sign on the internet and it is a key part of how I styled my website. I found similar fonts to get as close to the sign as possible and incorporated them into the website's header to give it that same vibrant and eye-catching look. (O'dwyer, 2021)

For the website's navigation, I was inspired by examples provided in class. I wanted the navigation to be user-friendly and easy to follow, much like the clear and simple layouts seen in well-designed websites.

I drew colour inspiration from a clothing website I visited recently, where I liked their bold and dynamic use of colour. I also liked how the buttons on that site worked, their interactive design encouraged me to use similar features, ensuring that the buttons on my website stood out and were easy to use.
